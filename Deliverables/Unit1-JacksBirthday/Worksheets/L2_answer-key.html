Teacher Answer Key: Lesson 2 RFS Worksheet -- Homographs and /b/ Sound

Lesson Plan Guidance: This key covers Word Work 1 (/b/ sound identification) and Word Work 2 (homograph matching) from Page 6. For Q2, accept any valid description of both meanings of 'box'. For Q3, accept 'both' as the correct answer for 'cover'.

📖 EXERCISE 1: READ, FIND AND WRITE — Answers
1. Does the word "bat" start with the /b/ sound?
The student should write: YES -- bat starts with /b/. Say it: /b/-/b/-bat!
Text Evidence: Word Work 1, Page 6 -- "bat" picture starts with /b/ and should be colored.
2. What are TWO meanings of the word "box"?
The student should write: box = a container with a lid / box = a sport (boxing)
Text Evidence: "He sees a big box in his room." (Page 5) -- box as container. Boxing = sport.
3. What does "cover" mean -- lid on top, front of a book, or both?
The student should write: BOTH -- cover = lid on top AND cover = front of a book
Text Evidence: "It is covered in brown paper." (Page 5) -- cover as lid/wrap.
🎨 EXERCISE 2: DRAW AND LABEL — Answer
Draw YOUR own /b/ word from real life!
The student's drawing should show: Any valid /b/ word drawn: ball, book, bus, banana, bird, bear, etc.
Label 1 (My /b/ word is:): Accept any /b/ word (ball, book, bus, banana, bird, etc.)
Label 2 (It starts with:): /b/ (the letter B)
